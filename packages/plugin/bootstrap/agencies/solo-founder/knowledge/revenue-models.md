# Revenue Models for Solo Founders

> Common monetization strategies ranked by solo-founder friendliness.

## Tier 1: Best for Solo (Low Ops Overhead)
| Model | Example | Pros | Cons |
|-------|---------|------|------|
| **SaaS subscription** | $10-50/mo | Predictable, scalable | Needs retention |
| **One-time purchase** | $29-199 | Simple, no churn | No recurring |
| **Freemium + Pro** | Free tier + $X/mo | Growth + revenue | Free tier costs |

## Tier 2: Good but More Work
| Model | Example | Pros | Cons |
|-------|---------|------|------|
| **Marketplace fee** | 5-15% cut | Network effects | Chicken-egg problem |
| **API usage** | $0.01/call | Scales with usage | Unpredictable revenue |
| **Sponsorship** | Newsletter ads | Low effort | Needs audience first |

## Tier 3: Avoid as Solo
- **Ad-supported** — needs massive scale
- **Enterprise sales** — needs sales team
- **Hardware** — logistics nightmare alone

## Pricing Psychology
- **3 tiers** — Free / Pro / Enterprise (anchor effect)
- **Annual discount** — 20% off = 2 months free (reduces churn)
- **Founder pricing** — Early adopters get lifetime discount (builds loyalty)
